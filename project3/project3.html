Responsive Design and CSS

Importance of responsive design and it's impact on the target audience

Responsive websites are all about user experience and interface when browsing a website streamlining responsive links, navigation, active links, etc. This in turn, increases interaction rate with your well-designed website becoming the content itself. If your website creates a positive user experience will notify search engines such as Google to boost search ratings. Another key factor of a responsive site, is to focus on multimedia platforms mainly desktop or mobile, suiting the user’s device when the use visits any page, the site can render accordingly. Elements of the website will change the size and location of each element and reorganizes making the visitor having the best visual experience flawlessly.

Once this is taken into consideration, the site can generate traffic showcasing ads, banners, images, links, or videos presented. Search Engines prefers responsive websites such as Google encouraging responsive websites for user’s results. Websites that have better performance and fast loading content makes it easier for users to share or link your content with single URL.

Describe three different kinds of reports that will tell you what information your target audinece is accessing

1. Audience Overview report provides an overall snapshot of the traffic visited through your website. You can see where your traffic is coming, length of engagement, and conversion rates of each source or content. This will also show the strength and effectiveness of your marketing strategy to further plan and execute strategies of potential development.

2. Behavior Flow report is useful to oversee and understand the popularity and the most/least engaged contents of your site from your incoming traffic. You can see the user’s flow through your website and the drop-off rate of each page. This can provide which pages to revise or remove determining the value to improve. Time tracking is also included in this report showcasing how long the users spends on your website, least/most impactful content of your website, and further finding the drop-off rates.

3. New vs Returning Visitors provides data of providing and understanding of who is coming to your website. You can analyze the potential different type of visitors and how they are becoming your customer. Customizing your variables will pinpoint making the data more relevant to your target audience helping you plan marketing effectively to specific types of visitors.
