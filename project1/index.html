SEO Explained

How SEO Works?

The letter of SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. SEOs improve traffic and visibility to websites with relevant searches. The better a website is visible in the search results, the better a website will receive new viewers and customers to wide array of information. Google is one of the most popular search engines on the web as a bot visiting multiple sites collecting information from those pages into an index. The algorithms then analyze from the index from all the data that has been gathered of ranking factors determines the order of pages appearing in the search results.

Importance of Keywords & Meta Tags

Metatags and Keywords have their difference and benefits. Keywords are not only part of the content but is also used to identify what the overall content is about. While Metatags are created by the originator of the content describing what the content is about and its relations to the content. Keywords identify what your is about and then the search engine knows what terms or phrases are highly searched by users and looks for those words and phrases on web pages. The search engine uses this information rank websites, blogs, and any other media content as also called Search Engine Optimization. Meta tags are snippets of code communicates with search engines of important information about websites, such as how they should display it in search results. They also tell web browsers how to display it to visitors.

The Four Pillars of SEO Strategy

Technical SEO: You need to ensure that search engine can read your content and explore your site. Some things to keep in mind in terms of designing your site. Crawl – Is your site explorable? Index – The clarity of which pages the search engine can index and return. Mobile – Is your site mobile friendly? Speed – Fast webpage loading times are recommended Tech – Is your search engine-friendly tech? Hierarchy – How is your content structured and organized on your websites? On-site SEO Optimization: Your website should be optimized on an individual page level. You must understand the language of your target audience by ensuring that your URL is simple and directive. The page titles should contain keywords and meta description to draw traffic and clicks. The site should be easy to use and navigate on what to do next. Content: Content is essential as it tells the main focal point of who, what, where, and why your content is showcased. All websites should consider the service content (what you do and where you do it), credibility content (why a viewer should engage with your site), and marketing content (content that helps your website in front of other websites). Off-Site Authority Building: Link-building is a large part of authority building that a crucial component to develop organic ranking and followings. Keep the links direct and simple. Create links that deserve attention and directed to relevant sources, demonstration, or point of direction.

Heading Hierarchy

Headings and phrases are placed in the beginning of sections to explain the overall summary of the topic in question. Headings come in six levels of H1 to H6. H1, H2, and H3 are heavily used in HTML’s pages. H1s are mainly used for “main heading” and H2-H6 are used for “subheadings” indicating sub-levels.
